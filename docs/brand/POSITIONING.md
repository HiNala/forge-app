# Forge — positioning (canonical)

This document is the source of truth for marketing, product copy, and lifecycle messaging. When in doubt, align to this file first.

**Human review:** Brian (or product owner) should sign off on changes here before large downstream rewrites (per V2-P01).

## One-liner (22 words)

Forge is the fastest way to build a **mini-app** — a form, a landing page, a proposal, a pitch deck, a website prototype, a mobile app design — describe it, ship it, share it, track it. Never touch a database.

## Tagline (3–6 words)

**Mini-apps in minutes** — short, specific, and testable in the hero. Alternates: “Build it. Ship it. Track it.” · “From idea to live link in five minutes.”

## Audience (priority)

1. **Solo professionals & small business owners** — contractors, consultants, photographers, tutors, freelancers — who need a custom form, proposal, or landing experience today.
2. **Founders & operators at small startups** — pitch decks, internal one-pagers, customer-facing forms — without a full-time designer or developer.
3. **Designers exploring ideas** — a fast canvas for mobile or web concepts and a clean handoff.
4. **Google Forms, Typeform, Calendly, Canva, or Carrd users** — Forge replaces several point tools with one workflow that is better at the branded, tracked outcome.

## Categories we compete in (and the real alternative)

| If they would have used… | Forge offers… |
|--------------------------|---------------|
| Google Forms / Typeform | A branded form with analytics and (where relevant) scheduling in one link. |
| Calendly / SavvyCal | Booking embedded in the same surface as the rest of the page — one link. |
| PandaDoc / Proposify | A proposal generated from a prompt, with read tracking in Forge. |
| Canva deck templates | Narrative and structure for a pitch deck with export when you need a file. |
| Carrd / one-page site builders | A hosted page with submission handling and analytics without wiring backends. |
| Figma (for ideation) | Fast generation from a prompt and export paths; not a Figma replacement for production design systems. |

## What we are explicitly NOT

- **Not a code editor** — not Cursor, Bolt, or Lovable for shipping arbitrary apps.
- **Not a full SaaS app builder** — no custom databases users manage, no arbitrary multi-tenant product logic.
- **Not a database product** — Forge stores what it must for hosting, forms, and analytics; users are not curating schemas.
- **Not Figma** — collaboration depth and design-system tooling stay in dedicated design tools.

## Voice

- **Specific over abstract** — “a fence-installation quote in three minutes” beats “10× your pipeline.”
- **Direct, not cold** — Lucy's helpful coworker, not a faceless “platform.”
- **Confident, not breathless** — we say what we do; we do not double every adjective.
- **Never** apologetic, robotic, or effusive.

## Three sample sentences (on-brand)

1. Describe your business. Forge builds the page. Share the link.
2. Your contact form, your proposal, your pitch deck — all built the same way, all tracked the same way.
3. When you outgrow it, take it with you. Until then, Forge handles everything.

## Terminology

- **Mini-app** — the user-facing name for a Forge artifact: a hosted, purpose-built surface (form, page, deck, etc.).
- **Page** — still valid in the UI for a specific item in the dashboard when “page” is clearer than “mini-app” (e.g. a single contact form’s detail view).
- **Workflow** — the type of thing being built: contact form, proposal, pitch deck, landing page, website, mobile design, etc.
